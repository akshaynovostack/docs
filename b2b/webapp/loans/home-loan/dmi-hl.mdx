**Step12: Select the Loan Provider with Offer Details**

On this screen, the DSA is presented with multiple loan provider options. Each card contains essential information such as:

* Provider Name: e.g., Piramal, DMI Housing Finance, ICICI, Motilal Oswal, etc.

  * ROI (Rate of Interest): The interest rate offered by the provider

  * FOIR (Fixed Obligation to Income Ratio): Percentage of income obligations

# **Step 13: Additional Details Submission Description:**

This step involves the borrower providing additional details required for processing their home loan application. The interface, as seen in the image, displays various fields capturing essential

information related to the applicant's financial and property details.

# **Key Requirements:**

1. **Aadhaar Number** – The borrower must input their Aadhaar number for identification and verification purposes.

2. **Property Identified?** – A dropdown option where the borrower confirms whether they have identified the property for which the loan is being sought.

3. **Property Type** – Selection of the type of property, such as a DA Authority Approved Map.

4. **Employee Sector Type** – Specifies the borrower’s employment sector, such as "Private Ltd".

5. **Property Value** – The estimated market value of the property, entered in Indian Rupees.

6. **Address Proof Type** – The document provided as proof of address, such as an Aadhaar UID Card.

7. **Property Address & Pincode** – The location details of the property, including address and postal code.

8. **Profile Segment Type** – Indicates the borrower’s financial segment, such as "Bank Salary".

9. **Net Income** – The borrower’s declared annual income, which helps in assessing loan eligibility.

10. **Deductions** – Shows any existing deductions (₹25,000), which may impact the loan repayment capacity.

11. **Expected Retirement Age** – This field captures the borrower’s anticipated retirement age (e.g., 58). It is crucial in determining the loan tenure, as lenders ensure that loan repayment aligns with the applicant’s active earning period.

12. **Purpose** – A dropdown field where the applicant selects the purpose of the loan, such as home purchase, construction, or renovation. This helps lenders categorize the loan appropriately.

13. **Branch Code** – Specifies the branch handling the loan application (e.g., "Digital Branch"). It ensures the application is processed through the correct financial institution channel.

14. **Sales Person** – A field to enter the sales representative or loan officer assisting with the application. This helps track the responsible agent for better service and follow-ups.  
15. 	**Continue Button** – A red button at the bottom that allows the user to proceed to the next step of the loan application process after all required details are filled.

# **Step 14: Document Upload for KYC and Conditional Documents**

In the loan application flow of the assisted journey, Step 14 focuses on the document submission process. This step involves uploading essential KYC and conditional documents for verification.

# **Details:**

1. **KYC Documents:**

   * Upload Aadhar Card

   * Upload PAN Card

2. # **Conditional Documents:**

   * Upload Last Year Salary Slip (only if applicable based on loan type)

3. # **Upload Form 16:**

   Form 16 is a tax document issued by employers to salaried employees in India. It serves as proof of the employee's income and the tax deducted at source (TDS).

4. # **Upload Bank Statement (Last 6 Months):**

   A bank statement provides a detailed account of the applicant's financial transactions and balances over a period of six months.

5. # **Address Proof:**

   Address proof documents are required to verify the applicant’s current residential address.
